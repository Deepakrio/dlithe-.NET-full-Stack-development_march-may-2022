About Holi

If you're wondering, "What is Holi?" you might find there isn't one straight answer to that question. Holi is a celebration of the end of winter and the beginning of spring. It's a celebration of rebirth and new beginnings, and a time to let the bad and the negative melt away. Holi is a Hindu festival that takes place every spring. It's a two-day holiday; the day of the March full moon is Holika Dahan. That's when a bonfire is lit for a puja (or prayer). The bonfire is cleansing and meant to burn away all the bad and evil. The next day, directly after the full moon, is all about the festival of colors.
